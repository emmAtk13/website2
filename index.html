Welcome

Hi! My name is Emma Atkinson

I'm a digital arts student based in New York. Currently, I am a senior at Clarkson University, dual majoring in Digital Arts & Sciences and Communication with a minor in mathematics.

I blend together the technical and artistic sides of new and emerging media, creating a unique experience. With a strong knowledge of coding (C++/C#/Java) and a solid design background, I work with the best of both worlds. I am interested in the haptic communication of virtual reality and games. Through animations and interactions, I focus on creating narratives as a means for experiences with the user. I like to explore the concept of communication and the connotations that people create through their experiences that shape their perception.
Specialties range from game design, virtual reality experiences, user experience design, graphic design, motion graphics, animation, film production and screenwriting.

(if) only you could understand (2019)

virtual reality and coding

(if) only you could understand

(if) only you could understand is a virtual reality poem by Emma Atkinson, whose passages are furthered by interactivity. Gesture-based interactions of words in these passages forwards the progression of each phrase. Words take on literal meaning, but can also take on connotative ideas through their interactivity. Actions trigger events through grabbing and touching. The monochromatic white look highlights the visual aspect of language via each floating stanza which evolves throughout the experience:

You Twist my words
Things Change
But I can only Bend so far
Days turn into Night
There is always More
I can feel the Distance
You can Break me
But only I can Destroy me

all the words (i never said) (2019)

virtual reality and coding

all the words (i never said)

An extension of the language study. all the words (i never said) is an interactive piece that looks at the physicality of words and breaks it down to its simplest structure. The perception of language at its simplest is letters, and the connection between the letter and its corresponding speech sound is a concept that can be broken at its basis.

in the dark (2017)

3D modeling in Maya

in the dark

This abstract video draws attention to the interplay and connection between light and dark. Even though it is a simulated environment, the relationship of light needing dark to be created and the lack of light creating darkness still exists. Unique and intriguing patterns are created and destroyed through this exchange.
